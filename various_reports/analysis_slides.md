# What to avoid

Do not overload the deck with:

* too many provider names on early slides
* too many raw hospital tables
* too many low-volume oddities
* too many caveats on every page

Put the caveats in one methodology slide, then move on.

---

# Suggested tone for the whole deck

Keep the commentary like this:

* factual
* directional
* operational
* not accusatory
* not overconfident

You want it to sound like:

> here is what the data strongly suggests, and here is where we should look next

not:

> gotcha, these people are doing it wrong

# Design guidance for the deck

## Format

* **16:9 widescreen**
* white background
* primary accent color: **RGB(0,40,80)** or hex **#002850**
* muted secondary palette:

  * slate blue
  * soft gray
  * light blue
  * restrained teal for emphasis

## Layout style

* one strong message per slide
* left-aligned headline
* one main visual
* compact observation box on the right or below
* minimal clutter
* direct labels where possible
* no more than 3 bullets per slide outside the chart

## McKinsey-style headline rule

Each title should read like a conclusion, not a topic.
Example:

* good: **Hospitalist group share varies materially by facility**
* weak: **Facility by group share**
