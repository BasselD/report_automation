## Recommended deck structure for Dr. T

Keep it to **7 slides max**. That is enough to show the story from multiple angles without turning it into an anatomy class.

The theme should be:

> **Who is getting the business, how are they behaving, and are there any early quality signals worth watching?**

---

# Slide 1. Executive summary

## Purpose

Start with the 3 to 5 biggest findings so he can see the answer immediately.

## What to show

A simple text slide with maybe one small summary table on the right.

### Include

* the report objective
* time period
* main methodology note:

  * **authorization events as backbone**
  * **hospitalist attribution via professional claims**
  * **Beyond + Benchmark bundled**
  * **provider volume reliability flag added**

## Suggested sections

### Header

**Hospitalist Utilization and Readmission Review. VOP 2025 with early 2026 directional refresh**

### 3 to 4 bullets

* Volume is concentrated by facility, with each major hospital largely dominated by one primary hospitalist group.
* At Harlingen Medical Center, Catalyst continues to hold the larger share of identified hospitalized volume versus the bundled Beyond/Benchmark view.
* Group-level observation rates are more interpretable after excluding low-volume noise and applying provider reliability thresholds.
* Readmission results are now directionally usable and should be interpreted alongside volume mix and facility context.

## Possible observation comment

**The analysis confirms that hospital routing patterns are not evenly distributed across groups, which makes facility-level group comparison highly actionable for operational and contracting discussions.**

---

# Slide 2. Methodology and interpretation guardrails

## Purpose

One clean slide so he remembers what he is looking at and does not over-interpret the wrong thing.

## What to show

A left-to-right process diagram or 4-box methodology summary.

### Boxes

1. **Event backbone**

   * observation + acute authorizations
2. **Hospitalist attribution**

   * first professional claim during stay
   * specialty funnel
3. **Group normalization**

   * Beyond + Benchmark bundled
   * Catalyst, H&I, Femi, etc.
4. **Reliability framework**

   * low / moderate / reliable provider volume indicator

## Add a small footnote

* Observation-to-acute conversions are bundled as acute in the authorization workflow.
* Provider-level outliers with very low counts should not be interpreted as stable operating patterns.

## Possible observation comment

**This version is designed to reduce noise by standardizing hospitalist groups and separating low-volume provider artifacts from more reliable volume-based comparisons.**

---

# Slide 3. Share of business by hospitalist group and facility

## Purpose

This is the core business slide. It should probably be the first real chart.

## What to show

**100% stacked bar chart** by facility with hospitalist groups as segments.

### Why

This directly answers:

* who is getting the business
* how concentrated each hospital is
* whether one group dominates

## Suggested title

**Hospitalist group share varies materially by facility, with some hospitals showing clear routing concentration**

## Possible observation comment

**Most major facilities appear to be predominantly served by one primary hospitalist group, while Harlingen Medical Center shows the clearest head-to-head split in the current VOP footprint.**

---

# Slide 4. Focused hospital comparison

## Purpose

Zoom in on the one or two facilities he cares about most.

## What to show

A **grouped bar chart or simple table screenshot** for:

* Harlingen Medical Center
* one other hospital of interest, if relevant

### Fields

* Group
* Acute
* Observation
* Total
* Share of facility volume

## Why

This is the slide where he can say:

* Catalyst got X
* Beyond/Benchmark got Y
* now I know who is driving admissions

## Suggested title

**At Harlingen Medical Center, Catalyst holds the larger share of identified hospitalized volume in the current comparison**

## Possible observation comment

**The Harlingen comparison provides the clearest operational view of competitive routing, with Catalyst currently accounting for the majority share relative to the bundled Beyond/Benchmark group.**

---

# Slide 5. Observation rate by hospitalist group and facility

## Purpose

Show utilization behavior, but keep it interpretable.

## What to show

Your **heatmap**, but annotate or footnote it with the reliability indicator.

### Best practice

Do not show raw provider-level outliers on this slide unless they are reliable volume.
Use:

* group level
* facility level
* maybe a small marker or note that low-volume cells should be interpreted cautiously

## Suggested title

**Observation rates are generally within a workable range at the group level, while extreme values are largely concentrated in low-volume combinations**

## Possible observation comment

**Once low-volume provider noise is separated out, most group-level observation rates appear much closer to expected operating ranges, with only a smaller set of facility-group combinations warranting deeper review.**

---

# Slide 6. Provider-level drilldown with reliability flag

## Purpose

Give him a way to inspect providers without overwhelming the main narrative.

## What to show

A **table screenshot** sorted by:

* hospitalist group
* facility
* reliable volume first
* then moderate
* then low

### Include columns

* Attending provider
* Group
* Facility
* Acute
* Observation
* Total
* Observation rate
* Reliability flag

### Why

This lets him see:

* which individual providers are driving the group
* whether an outlier is real or just low volume
* where deeper follow-up might be needed

## Suggested title

**Provider-level variation is best interpreted through the reliability lens, with most extreme rates driven by low-volume providers**

## Possible observation comment

**The reliability indicator helps distinguish meaningful provider patterns from small-number distortion, making the drilldown more practical for follow-up conversations.**

---

# Slide 7. Readmissions and next actions

## Purpose

Close with the quality angle and the follow-up path.

## What to show

One simple table or bar chart with:

* group
* acute volume
* readmission count
* readmission rate

If you can, also show:

* facility or group comparisons for readmission
* but keep it simple, because readmissions can turn into a swamp fast

## Important

Frame it as:

* useful signal
* not final scorecard of physician performance in isolation

## Suggested title

**Readmission patterns now provide a usable directional signal and should be interpreted alongside volume, facility mix, and utilization patterns**

## Possible observation comment

**With the corrected logic in place, readmissions can now be used as a directional quality indicator, particularly when reviewed in combination with facility share and observation behavior rather than as a standalone metric.**

# Optional appendix slide

If you think he may ask questions, include one appendix slide but do not lead with it.

## Appendix. Definitions

### Include

* Observation rate = Observation / (Observation + Acute)
* Share of business = group total / facility total
* Readmission rate definition
* Volume reliability thresholds
* Beyond + Benchmark bundling rule

This saves you from explaining it three times live.

# Best presentation order

Use this flow:

1. **Executive summary**
2. **Methodology / guardrails**
3. **Share of business by facility**
4. **Focused Harlingen comparison**
5. **Observation rate heatmap**
6. **Provider drilldown with reliability**
7. **Readmissions and next actions**

That gives you:

* business ownership
* methodology trust
* utilization pattern
* provider nuance
* quality signal

---

# What to avoid

Do not overload the deck with:

* too many provider names on early slides
* too many raw hospital tables
* too many low-volume oddities
* too many caveats on every page

Put the caveats in one methodology slide, then move on.

---

# Suggested tone for the whole deck

Keep the commentary like this:

* factual
* directional
* operational
* not accusatory
* not overconfident

You want it to sound like:

> here is what the data strongly suggests, and here is where we should look next

not:

> gotcha, these people are doing it wrong

---

# One simple template for your observation comments

For each slide, use this pattern:

**What the chart shows**
**Why it matters**
**What to do next**

Example:
**Catalyst holds the larger share of volume at Harlingen Medical Center, confirming that hospital-level routing is not evenly distributed across competing groups. This matters because it affects preferred provider strategy and hospital conversations. Next step is to monitor whether this share shifts over time and whether readmission or observation patterns differ meaningfully across groups.**

---

# My recommendation for the screenshot style

Since you are using screenshots in PowerPoint:

* crop tightly
* use only one main chart per slide
* add 2 to 3 bullets on the right
* add one **bold observation sentence** under the title
* gray out anything not central
* if a table is too wide, only show top rows and mention “full detail available”
