Oliver Meeting Cheat Sheet

Opening

I want to make sure we support the work effectively while also protecting the transition from Patrick. My goal today is to clarify the deliverable, confirm what already exists, and agree on ownership.

Start With What Already Exists

* Andrew’s Stars projections and gap-closure report
* Amber’s prioritization spreadsheet
* Existing outreach and risk scores
* Existing attest dashboard
* Patrick’s Epic, RPO, and readmission analyses

Ask:

What is materially missing from these existing assets?

Clarify the Ask

* What exact deliverable are you expecting?
* Is this a report, dataset, dashboard, prototype, or presentation?
* Who will use it?
* What decision or action will it support?
* What does “complete” look like?
* Which items are required now versus later?

Define Ownership

Since Amber and Stephanie already appear aligned with the desired methodology, can they lead the primary build while our team supports data sourcing, validation, and technical gaps?

* Innovista: business requirements, workflow, prototype, implementation
* Our team: data expertise, validation, targeted technical support
* New asks route through me for capacity review

Avoid Duplicate Work

* Which prior analyses are already sufficient to move forward?
* Are we recreating anything already answered?
* What additional analysis could realistically change the decision?
* Can we pilot the current conclusion instead of continuing discovery?

Protect the Transition

Patrick is leaving, and Yuzi and Andrew C are absorbing critical responsibilities. We need to be deliberate about their capacity.

* Knowledge transfer remains the immediate priority
* Avoid assigning both resources to every meeting
* Avoid open-ended discovery
* New work requires clear priority and tradeoffs

Close With Decisions

Before ending, confirm:

1. What are we building?
2. What already exists?
3. What is still missing?
4. Who owns each component?
5. What is the deadline?
6. What is explicitly out of scope?

Key Line

I’m not trying to reduce support. I’m trying to make sure our support is targeted, nonduplicative, and connected to a clear business action.

* What specific product, report, dataset, or workflow he expects
* What decision it will support
* Who will use it
* What action should occur because of it
* What “finished” looks like
